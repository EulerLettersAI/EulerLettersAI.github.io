About

Who we are

Euler Letters AI is led by Samer El Kababji, Assistant Professor at Princess Sumaya University for Technology (PSUT). The organization hosts multidisciplinary repositories for research projects and, occasionally, selected MSc and capstone work—especially projects that benefit from open methods, reproducible pipelines, and clear documentation.

We keep sensitive datasets out of public repositories and publish responsibly (especially in medical contexts).

What we value

  • Clarity — readable code, structured documentation, reproducible experiments.
  • Interdisciplinarity — AI as a bridge between fields, not a silo.
  • Open scholarship — tools and insights that others can learn from and build on.
  • Practical impact — methods that survive real constraints (data, compute, deployment).

Why “Euler Letters AI”

Inspiration

The name is inspired by Leonhard Euler’s Letters to a German Princess—a remarkable collection where he explains ideas across mathematics, physics, astronomy, and philosophy in a disciplined, teachable way.

What stood out to me is how Euler connects concepts across domains while staying systematic. That habit—linking the dots without losing rigor—is exactly what modern AI teams need.

Continuity of knowledge

“Euler Letters AI” also reflects a broader idea: the advancement of knowing the world is a long conversation across civilizations and generations. We aim to honor that continuity by building modern AI systems that are technically sound, ethically aware, and genuinely useful.

In other words: we treat AI as part of an ongoing scientific tradition—not a shortcut around thinking.

Lab objective

Methods

Develop reproducible AI pipelines: data engineering, model development, evaluation, interpretability, and deployment-minded experimentation.

Applications

Apply AI across engineering and medical contexts, including time series, signals, sensor systems, predictive modeling, and decision support—always with domain constraints in mind.

Open scholarship

Publish clean repositories, tutorials, and teaching-grade examples that help students and researchers learn by building.

Euler as a teacher across disciplines

Short excerpts

“I shall endeavor to explain everything with the greatest possible clarity…” — Euler, Letters to a German Princess
“We must not confuse what we imagine with what is demonstrated.” — Euler, Letters to a German Princess

These short pull quotes reflect the tone we aim for: clear explanations, disciplined reasoning, and respect for evidence—whether the topic is physical, mathematical, or conceptual.

What this means in the AI era

Today, AI projects often sit at the intersection of multiple domains: algorithms, statistics, data engineering, ethics, and the application field itself.

We adopt a “letters mindset”: explain assumptions, define terms, document reasoning, and connect methods to reality. This is how results become trustworthy and reusable—not just impressive demos.
